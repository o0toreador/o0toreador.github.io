Tonkatsu Ramen Recipe

  • Coconut Curry Broth


    1. Toast the curry paste in a small amount of oil until fragrant.


    2. Add the stock and coconut milk, stirring to dissolve the paste. Cook at a steady simmer for about 5 minutes.


    3. Season with fish sauce and lime juice, if desired.

  • Egg Noodles


    Egg noodles range from thin and curly to wide and flat, and are sold fresh or dried; all but the very heftiest and heartiest - think lo mein - are suited to noodle soup.


    They should be cooked as briefly as possible - no more than a minute if they're fresh, and slightly longer if dried - to preserve that bouncy bite.

  • Pork Tonkatsu


    1. Season pork with salt and pepper. Dust both sides evenly with flour, tapping off excess.


    2. Dip the meat in a bowl of beaten egg. (Allow 1 egg for every 2 pork chops.)


    3. Coat the meat evenly in panko, pressing down gently to make sure it sticks.


    4. Heat a heavy skillet with about 1/2 inch of vegetable oil to 350Â°F. Using kitchen tongs, lay 1 or 2 cutlets in the oil and deep-fry until golden brown (about 7 minutes), turning once or twice. Drain on paper towels and slice before serving.

  • Broccoli


    Blanch florets in well-salted boiling water until broccoli is still firm but can be pierced with a fork (about 3 minutes). Drain and use right away, or toss in any or all of the following: sesame oil, oyster sauce, toasted sesame seeds.

  • Sriracha


    This much-loved hot sauce needs no introduction, but it's worth noting that when we use sriracha to add heat to a dish, we are also adding salt, sugar, and acidity. If you've put a lot of thought into seasoning your broth, you might want to consider a more neutral heat agent: chili oil, say, or straight up chopped chilis.
